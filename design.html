For this AEJMC logo contest, each of the students in the class entered our final projects of these designs. This contest is one that takes place every year for the AEJMC. The semester that I completed this, was for the upcoming conference in San Francisco that following year. I took inspiration from the geographic layout of the city San Francisco and elements that are specific to the area, like the trolley’s. We used InDesign and Illustrator for this project, and each one was unique to us, but each student completed a logo design on their own.

This project was completed for a client that my capstone class worked with. The ask from the client was to redesign the landing page of their ‘Water Quality Monitoring’ information section of the website. Obviously, that came with more than just the one task. Our entire class went above and beyond to deliver the client with content including short/long form video, social media templates, infographics, high fidelity wireframes for the page, and photographs. My specific territory of the deliverables was infographics. The final draft that I submitted for my infographic is intended for presentation form use. We had originally mocked up the graphics to be used on the website, but the client changed their mind. I used Adobe Illustrator to build this infographic and each element that makes up for it.
